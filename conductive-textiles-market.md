<p><p>Conductive textiles can be categorized into three main types: woven, non-woven, and knitted textiles. Woven textiles involve interlacing yarns to create a structured fabric, suitable for applications requiring durability and strength. Non-woven textiles are manufactured by bonding fibers together, offering flexibility and lightweight properties ideal for disposable applications. Knitted textiles, created by interlocking loops of yarn, provide stretch and comfort, making them suitable for fashion and wearable tech. Each type serves unique applications in various industries.</p></p>
