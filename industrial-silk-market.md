<p><p>The industrial silk market comprises several types. Real silk is derived from silkworms and is prized for its quality and luster. Synthetic silk, made from petroleum-based fibers, mimics the feel of real silk at a lower cost. Tussah silk comes from wild silkworms, offering a coarser texture. Artificial silk, like rayon, is manufactured from cellulose and shares some characteristics of real silk. Interwoven silk combines silk with other materials for added durability. Other types may include specialty silks with unique properties.</p></p>
