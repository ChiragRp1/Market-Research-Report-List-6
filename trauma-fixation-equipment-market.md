Internal Fixation includes groups such as Plates, Screws, Nails, and other devices used to stabilize fractured bones internally. External Fixation involves the use of external devices like rods, pins, and frames to immobilize and align fractured bones from outside the body. These fixation methods are crucial in the treatment of traumatic injuries to ensure proper healing and restoration of bone function.</p></p>
