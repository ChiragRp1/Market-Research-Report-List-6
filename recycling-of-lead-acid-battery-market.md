<p><p>The recycling of lead-acid batteries involves two main market types: echelon utilization and raw material recycling. Echelon utilization refers to the practice of reusing batteries in secondary applications, extending their lifecycle before full recycling. In contrast, raw material recycling focuses on the recovery of valuable materials, such as lead and sulfuric acid, from used batteries. This process not only reduces environmental impact but also conserves resources by allowing these materials to be repurposed in new battery production and other industries.</p></p>
