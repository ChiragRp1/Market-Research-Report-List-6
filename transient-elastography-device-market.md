<p><p>The Transient Elastography device market comprises two main types: Vibration Controlled Transient Elastography (VCTE) and Controlled Attenuation Parameter (CAP). VCTE measures liver stiffness to assess fibrosis by analyzing shear wave velocity, while CAP quantifies hepatic steatosis by evaluating ultrasound attenuation. Both technologies offer non-invasive alternatives to traditional biopsy, enhancing patient comfort and diagnostic accuracy. The growing prevalence of liver diseases and the demand for reliable screening methods drive the expansion of these innovative elastography techniques in clinical settings.</p></p>
