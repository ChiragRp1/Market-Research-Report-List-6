<p><p>The enzyme market for pulp and paper encompasses several types, each serving unique functions in the production process. Amylase enhances starch processing and improves paper strength. Cellulase breaks down cellulose, aiding in fiber separation and refining. Xylanase targets hemicellulose, enhancing pulp yield and brightness while reducing energy consumption. Lipase helps in removing fatty deposits, improving paper quality. Other enzymes include pectinase and lignase, which assist in various stages of pulping and bleaching, optimizing efficiency and sustainability in paper production.</p></p>
