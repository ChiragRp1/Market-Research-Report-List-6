<p><p>The PVDF membrane market is segmented into hydrophobic and hydrophilic types, each serving distinct applications. Hydrophobic PVDF membranes repel water, making them ideal for gas filtration and as barriers in various industrial processes. In contrast, hydrophilic PVDF membranes attract water, facilitating liquid filtration in applications like water treatment and biotechnology. Both types leverage PVDF's chemical resistance and thermal stability, promoting their use in diverse sectors, including pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, and environmental industries.</p></p>
