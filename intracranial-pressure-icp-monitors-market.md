<p><p>The intracranial pressure (ICP) monitors market comprises two primary types: invasive and non-invasive devices. Invasive monitors involve surgical placement of sensors within the cranial cavity to provide direct and accurate ICP measurements, often used in critical care settings. Non-invasive monitors utilize external technologies, such as transcranial Doppler ultrasound or optical methods, to estimate ICP without surgery. Both types are vital for diagnosing and managing conditions like traumatic brain injuries and neurological disorders, catering to different clinical needs and patient safety concerns.</p></p>
