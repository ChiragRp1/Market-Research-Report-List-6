<p><p>The Near Field Sensor market encompasses various types of sensors that operate at close range. Inductive sensors detect metallic objects using electromagnetic fields, while capacitive sensors sense changes in capacitance, detecting both metallic and non-metallic materials. Magnetic sensors respond to magnetic fields, and photoelectric sensors use light beams to identify objects. Ultrasonic sensors emit sound waves to determine distance and presence, while optical sensors detect light to gauge object proximity. Each type serves distinct applications across industries, enhancing automation and precision.</p></p>
