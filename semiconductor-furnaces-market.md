<p><p>The semiconductor furnace market comprises two primary types: vertical and horizontal furnaces. Vertical furnaces are characterized by their design, where substrates are placed in a vertical orientation, allowing for efficient heating and uniform temperature distribution, ideal for processes like CVD and diffusion. Horizontal furnaces, on the other hand, utilize a horizontal layout, making them suitable for batch processing and environments requiring larger substrate sizes. Each type serves specific applications within semiconductor manufacturing, catering to diverse production needs.</p></p>
