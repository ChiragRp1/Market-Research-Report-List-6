<p><p>The gasoline fuel pumps market comprises two primary types: mechanical and electric fuel pumps. Mechanical fuel pumps, often used in older vehicles, rely on engine motion to generate fuel pressure, providing a simple and reliable solution. In contrast, electric fuel pumps are commonly found in modern vehicles, utilizing electric motors to deliver fuel efficiently and at higher pressures. These pumps are typically more versatile and can support advanced fuel injection systems, driving their growing popularity in the automotive industry.</p></p>
