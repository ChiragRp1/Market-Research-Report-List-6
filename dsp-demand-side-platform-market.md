<p><p>Demand-Side Platforms (DSPs) are platforms used by advertisers to purchase digital ad inventory in real-time. There are two main market types within DSPs: Real-Time Bidding (RTB) and Programmatic Premium Buying (PPB). RTB involves the automated buying and selling of ad impressions in real-time auctions, allowing advertisers to bid on individual impressions based on data and targeting. PPB, on the other hand, involves the automated buying and selling of premium ad inventory through direct deals, allowing advertisers to secure guaranteed impressions at fixed prices.</p></p>
